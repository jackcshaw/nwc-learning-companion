# Claims Map

Use this file when the reader asks what the essay claims or wants to inspect the evidence. Keep the claim list short so the reader can choose where to go deeper.

## Claim 1: AI changes what artifacts prove

Polished papers, briefs, and analytic products no longer prove as much about the student's reasoning because AI can produce the visible artifact.

- **Best evidence:** the essay's discussion of artifact evidence, false learning, and assessment after automation.
- **Strongest unresolved question:** which artifacts still provide good evidence of learning when paired with process evidence or oral defense?
- **Where to look:** `nwc-human-machine-teaming-companion-draft.md`, sections "The Artifact No Longer Carries the Same Evidence" and "Assessment After Automation."

## Claim 2: NWC already teaches the competency AI makes urgent

Frame ownership is not a new AI skill. It overlaps with NWC's existing emphasis on strategic logic, assumptions, ends, ways, means, risk, and reassessment.

- **Best evidence:** the NWC Primer and the essay section "NWC Already Teaches the Competency AI Makes Urgent."
- **Strongest unresolved question:** how much of this is already assessable in current assignments, and where does AI require new evidence?
- **Where to look:** `sources/source-spine.md`.

## Claim 3: Access is not apprenticeship

Giving students AI access does not teach them the expert moves behind AI-enabled judgment. Faculty must make framing, assumption-testing, reliance, and critique visible.

- **Best evidence:** cognitive apprenticeship literature and Greg Ceccarelli's "Apprenticeship Was the Point."
- **Strongest unresolved question:** which parts of current NWC pedagogy already apprentice students into frame ownership, and which need redesign?
- **Where to look:** `sources/source-spine.md` and the essay section "Access Is Not Apprenticeship Into a Practice."

## Claim 4: AI makes visible competence cheap

AI can cheaply produce summaries, outlines, plausible options, polished prose, and balanced counterarguments. Those outputs remain useful, but they no longer prove the same competence.

- **Best evidence:** Sarah Guo's "The Untrainable," the jagged frontier study, and the essay section "What AI Makes Cheap."
- **Strongest unresolved question:** where does AI-generated competence actually improve learning rather than conceal shallow ownership?
- **Where to look:** `sources/source-spine.md`.

## Claim 5: Appropriate reliance is the right target

The educational target is not trust or distrust. Students need to learn when to rely, when to verify, when to redirect, and when to refuse the frame.

- **Best evidence:** Raees and Papangelis on appropriate reliance and the essay section "The Jagged Frontier and Appropriate Reliance."
- **Strongest unresolved question:** what level of reliance evidence is enough without turning assignments into bureaucracy?
- **Where to look:** `artifacts/traceable-learning-artifact.md`.

## Claim 6: The frame is not the framer

Even if AI can compare and revise frames, it still optimizes toward a purpose, signal, or constraint someone supplied, accepted, or allowed to govern the work.

- **Best evidence:** the essay section "The Frame Is Not the Framer."
- **Strongest unresolved question:** what changes if future systems begin to generate ends rather than optimize goals supplied by people or institutions?
- **Where to look:** `prompts/objections-and-responses.md`.

## Claim 7: Developmental friction must be designed, not accidentally erased

Some friction is waste, but some friction is how judgment forms. Faculty should decide which friction to preserve and which to automate.

- **Best evidence:** Ceccarelli's "Apprenticeship Was the Point" and the essay section "The Developmental Role of Friction."
- **Strongest unresolved question:** how can faculty preserve developmental struggle without needlessly increasing workload?
- **Where to look:** `cases/cyber-group-strategy-transfer-case.md`.

## Claim 8: The output should be a traceable learning artifact

AI-enabled work should leave evidence of frame choices, assumptions, reliance decisions, rejected outputs, oral-defense questions, and faculty review.

- **Best evidence:** the essay sections "From Tacit Judgment to Traceable Artifacts" and "What Students Should Have to Show."
- **Strongest unresolved question:** which trace elements are essential, and which become compliance theater?
- **Where to look:** `artifacts/traceable-learning-artifact.md`.
